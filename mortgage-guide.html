The PITI Breakdown

Most mortgage payments consist of four parts, commonly referred to as PITI:

P

Principal

The amount going toward paying down your actual loan balance

I

Interest

The cost of borrowing money from the lender

T

Taxes

Property taxes collected by your lender for local government

I

Insurance

Homeowners insurance and possibly PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance)

How Mortgage Interest Works

Mortgage interest is typically compounded monthly, which means you pay interest on both your principal balance and any accumulated interest.

Monthly Interest = (Annual Rate ÷ 12) × Remaining Principal

Amortization: The Magic of Mortgage Payments

In the early years of your mortgage, most of your payment goes toward interest. As time passes, more goes toward principal. This process is called amortization.

Example: On a 30-year $300,000 mortgage at 4%:

  • Year 1: $1,000 payment = $900 interest, $100 principal
  • Year 15: $1,000 payment = $600 interest, $400 principal

Additional Mortgage Costs to Consider

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

If your down payment is less than 20%, lenders typically require PMI. This protects the lender if you default on the loan and usually costs 0.5% to 1% of the loan amount annually.

Homeowners Insurance

Most lenders require insurance to protect their investment. Costs vary by location, home value, and coverage level.

Property Taxes

Lenders often collect 1/12 of your annual property tax bill each month, holding it in an escrow account.

Fixed-Rate vs. Adjustable-Rate Mortgages

Fixed-Rate Mortgages

Your interest rate and payment remain the same for the entire loan term. Perfect for stability and long-term planning.

Adjustable-Rate Mortgages (ARMs)

Start with a lower rate that adjusts periodically. Better for short-term ownership but carries uncertainty.

How Down Payments Affect Your Payment

Your down payment significantly impacts your monthly payment and total loan cost:

$400,000 Home | 20% Down = $320,000 Loan
$400,000 Home | 10% Down = $360,000 Loan + PMI
$400,000 Home | 5% Down = $380,000 Loan + Higher PMI
