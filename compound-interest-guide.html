What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest from previous periods. In simple terms, it's "interest on interest" that causes wealth to grow exponentially over time.

Simple Interest

Interest earned only on the original principal

$1,000 × 5% = $50/year
Compound Interest

Interest earned on principal + accumulated interest

Year 1: $1,000 × 5% = $50
Year 2: $1,050 × 5% = $52.50

The Compound Interest Formula

Compound interest is calculated using this mathematical formula:

A = P (1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:

  • A = Future value of the investment
  • P = Principal investment amount
  • r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
  • n = Number of times interest compounds per year
  • t = Number of years the money is invested

Key Factors That Affect Compound Growth

1. Time: Your Greatest Ally

The longer your money compounds, the more dramatic the growth. Starting early is the single most important factor in building wealth through compounding.

2. Rate of Return

Small differences in interest rates create huge differences over time. Earning 8% instead of 6% can double your ending balance over 30 years.

3. Compounding Frequency

How often interest is calculated and added to your balance:

  • Annual: Once per year
  • Quarterly: 4 times per year
  • Monthly: 12 times per year
  • Daily: 365 times per year

The Rule of 72: Quick Mental Math

Use this simple rule to estimate how long it takes your money to double:

72 ÷ Interest Rate = Years to Double

Examples:

  • 6% return: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years to double
  • 8% return: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years to double
  • 12% return: 72 ÷ 12 = 6 years to double

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not Starting Early Enough

Every year you delay costs you significantly in long-term growth potential.

2. Underestimating Small Amounts

Even small regular contributions grow substantially over time due to compounding.

3. Frequent Withdrawals

Taking money out interrupts the compounding process and dramatically reduces final results.
