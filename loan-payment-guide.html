The Loan Payment Formula

Loan payments are calculated using a standard mathematical formula that takes into account three key factors:

M = P [ r(1+r)^n ] / [ (1+r)^n - 1 ]

Where:

  • M = Monthly payment
  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12)
  • n = Number of payments (loan term in months)

Example Calculation

Let's say you want to calculate payments for a $20,000 car loan at 5% annual interest for 5 years (60 months):

P = $20,000
r = 5% ÷ 12 = 0.004167
n = 5 × 12 = 60 months
M = $377.42

Factors That Affect Your Loan Payments

1. Loan Amount (Principal)

The total amount you borrow directly impacts your monthly payment. Larger loans mean higher payments, but spreading payments over a longer term can make them more manageable.

2. Interest Rate

Your interest rate is determined by your credit score, loan type, and market conditions. Even a small difference in interest rates can significantly impact your total payment amount.

3. Loan Term

Longer loan terms mean lower monthly payments but higher total interest costs. Shorter terms have higher payments but less interest overall.

Types of Loan Payment Structures

Amortized Loans

Most common for mortgages and auto loans. Payments are fixed, but the portion going toward principal vs. interest changes over time.

Interest-Only Loans

You pay only interest for a set period, then begin paying principal. Common in some mortgage products.

Balloon Payments

Small regular payments with a large final payment. Useful for specific business or investment scenarios.

Common Loan Calculation Mistakes to Avoid

1. Forgetting Additional Fees

Many loans include origination fees, insurance, or other costs that aren't reflected in the principal amount.

2. Ignoring Compound Frequency

Some loans compound interest daily or quarterly rather than monthly, which affects the total cost.

3. Overlooking Prepayment Options

Some lenders allow extra payments that can significantly reduce your total interest paid.
